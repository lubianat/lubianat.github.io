A Multilingual Cell Ontology based on Wikidata

Source: LinkedIn
Date: 2024-07-15

As academics, we often find resources available only in English. While a common language is essential, our mother tongues are equally important, and it's empowering when platforms consider them. Notably, ontologies used for biomedical data annotation, like Gene Ontology, Disease Ontology, and UBERON anatomy ontology, are typically English-only. But what if we need standard labels in Portuguese, German, or Italian?

Wikidata supports over 200 languages for scientific concepts, evolving through community feedback and promoting epistemic justice. While resources like The Monarch Initiative's Human Phenotype Ontology are making great advances in internationalization (link), Wikidata presents an alternative, continuous community-driven system for other ontologies. By embracing Wikidata’s multilingual capabilities, we can democratize access to scientific knowledge.

It is particularly useful for the Human Cell Atlas (HCA) community, which already values so much human diversity. Wikidata’s multilingual capability offers a solution to internationalize its English-centric products, helping globalize cell biology advancements and promoting language diversity.

As the Cell Ontology is the de facto common registry for cell types, I’ve been exploring over the past four years how Wikidata can be harnessed for its advance. Yesterday I finally got to finish a multilingual prototype for the Cell Ontology, including cell type names in several languages. Check it out here: Multilingual Cell Ontology Prototype.
