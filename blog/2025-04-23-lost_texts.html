Lost texts around the web
Date: 2025-04-23

I have many lost blog-like texts around the web. Now I am trying to recover them.

I have written stuff on Medium (mostly in pt-br).

I have written stuff on a blog called "Points to Dots".

I have written stuff around Wikimedia pages (pt-br).

I have posted one little journal post on iNaturalist (pt-br).

I have written stuff on LinkedIn too

I have written >1000 Wikipedia articles in the Portuguese Wikipedia, but that maybe doesn't count.

I have written a PhD thesis, what is basically a long blog post.

I've had many posts on Facebook and Twitter, but I burned them on a little warm firepit. They may be found only in the guts of LLMs.

I live in a somewhat chaotic state of impermanence. I try and do too much stuff at the same time, and hosting a proper blog, well, I haven't been able to do it.

I am not tech-savvy enough for a super blog, nor quite willing to pay to corps and get vendor lock-in for hosting images and text.

Other than that, topics change, communities change. Exploration exploration exploration.

I write mostly about knowledge graphs and the Wikimedia ecosystem, but sometimes I go into the biomedical side of things, or the ontology side of things, or the meta-science side of things, or the biodiversity side of things (which happens to be what I really like to think about).

Bursts of writing. Streams of consciousness. Niche tutorials.

Sometimes in English, that is my working language.
Sometimes in Portuguese, a language I miss so much.

English has taken over my thoughts and my web presence and my writing hands

I've written prose and poems in Portuguese, like a 48-page travel diary to Colombia. It is not bad, but not quite a book.

My current attempt is a simple HTML blog, a simple structure. It is old-fashioned, I could say. It is the internet before it died. A person on a keyboard talking to the world wide web at https://tiago.bio.br/blog.

No ads, no algorithms. Just me, and GitHub Copilot putting unwanted words for tab-completing.

And the freebies of GitHub Pages.
